Dialogs

Modals, referred to as 'dialogs' in Angular, inform users about a task and contain critical information, require decisions or involve multiple tasks.

Modals appear in front of app or website content and disable all app or website functionality when they appear. They remain on screen until confirmed, dismissed or a required action has been taken.

Modals are purposefully interruptive, so they should be used sparingly.

Principles

Focussed - Modals focus user attention to ensure their content is addressed

Direct - Modals should be direct in communicating information and dedicated to completing a task

Helpful - Modals should appear in response to a user task or and action, with relevant contextual information

When to use

Modals should be used for:

  • Errors that block an app or website's normal operation
  • Critical information that requires a specific user task, decision or acknowledgement
